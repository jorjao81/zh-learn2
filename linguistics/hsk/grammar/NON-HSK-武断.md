# Grammar Point: 武断 (wǔduàn)

| Category | Chinese | Pinyin | Meaning | Part of Speech |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|----------------|
| Vocabulary | 武断 | wǔduàn | arbitrary; subjective; dogmatic | Adjective |

## 1. Core Semantics: The Subjective Judgment

**武断** describes a judgment or decision made subjectively, without sufficient evidence, or based purely on personal opinion. It carries a distinct **negative connotation**.

* **Literal logic:** "Martial decision" → deciding by force/will rather than reason.
* **Key synonyms:** 主观 (subjective - neutral/neg), 独断 (arbitrary/dictatorial).
* **Key antonyms:** 客观 (objective), 审慎 (prudent).

## 2. The "Twin" Contrast: 武断 vs. 果断

This is the most critical linguistic distinction for this word. Both describe quick, firm decisions, but the *basis* and *social value* are opposite.

| Word | Meaning | Basis of Action | Connotation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **果断** (guǒduàn) | Decisive | Courage + Insight | **Positive** (Compliment) |
| **武断** (wǔduàn) | Arbitrary | Subjectivity + Arrogance | **Negative** (Criticism) |

**Diagnostic Test:**
* If a leader makes a quick choice that saves the company, they are **果断**.
* If a leader makes a quick choice ignoring the data and experts, they are **武断**.

## 3. Syntactic Usage

### A. As a Predicative Adjective
It is most commonly used to criticize a statement or person.

> 你这样说是很**武断**的。
> *Nǐ zhèyàng shuō shì hěn wǔduàn de.*
> Saying it like that is very **arbitrary**.
> 下结论别太**武断**。
> *Xià jiélùn bié tài wǔduàn.*
> Don't be too **rash/subjective** when drawing conclusions.

### B. As an Adverbial Modifier
Modifies verbs of cognition (thinking, concluding, denying).

> 他**武断**地认为我是错的。
> *Tā wǔduàn de rènwéi wǒ shì cuò de.*
> He **arbitrarily** determined that I was wrong. (Implies he didn't check the facts).
> 不要**武断**地否定别人的建议。
> *Búyào wǔduàn de fǒudìng biérén de jiànyì.*
> Do not **dogmatically** reject others' suggestions.

## 4. Cultural/Pragmatic Context

In Chinese culture, which values harmony and consensus, accusing someone of being `武断` is a relatively sharp criticism. It implies they are lacking in `修养` (cultivation) because they are imposing their will rather than observing reality (`客观`).

However, in academic or logical debates, it is a standard term to point out a logical fallacy (asserting a conclusion without premises).

> **Common pattern:** "Pending further investigation, it would be **arbitrary (武断)** to conclude that X caused Y."

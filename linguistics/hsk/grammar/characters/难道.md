**难** (nán): difficult / hard / disaster

- **Etymology**: Originally a bird trapped or in distress. Meaning evolved to "difficult" or "adversity."
- **In Context**: Here it implies "It is hard to believe/accept."

**道** (dào): way / path / to say / reason

- **Meaning**: Often means "to speak" (e.g., 说道) or "reason/logic" (道理).
- **Combination**: **难道** literally suggests "Is it hard to say/admit...?" or "Does reason find it hard...?"
  - It signals that the proposition following it is "hard to swallow" or logical absurdity.

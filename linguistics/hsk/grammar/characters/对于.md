**对** (duì): facing / correct / to treat / regarding

- **Etymology**: The simplified form combines **又** (right hand) and **寸** (thumb/inch, representing rules/standards). Originally meant "to answer" or "to respond" (to match a standard), which evolved into "facing" or "directing action toward" a subject.
- **Function**: Establishes the directional relationship between the subject and the object.

**于** (yú): in / at / to / from / by

- **Etymology**: A primary preposition in Classical Chinese.
- **Function**: Formalizes the preposition, acting as the link that points the "facing" (对) toward the specific topic or person.

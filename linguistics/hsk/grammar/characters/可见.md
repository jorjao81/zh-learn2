# Character Breakdown: 可见 (kějiàn)

The word **可见** is a transparent compound, meaning its meaning is directly derived from its parts. Understanding the parts makes the grammar point (inference) intuitive: you aren't just saying "therefore," you are saying "This fact allows us to **see** that..."

## 1. 可 (kě)

* **Meaning**: Can, may, able to.
* **Role**: Potential modality.
* **Common Words**:
  * **可**能 (kěnéng) - Possible ("can be")
  * **可**以 (kěyǐ) - Can / May
  * **可**爱 (kě'ài) - Cute ("can be loved")

## 2. 见 (jiàn)

* **Meaning**: To see, to meet, to perceive.
* **Role**: Perception verb.
* **Common Words**:
  * 看**见** (kànjiàn) - To see (result of looking)
  * **见**面 (jiànmiàn) - To meet
  * 意**见** (yìjiàn) - Opinion ("meaning/intent view")

## Synthesis

**可见** = "Can [be] seen"

In Classical Chinese and formal Modern Chinese, **可 + Verb** often forms a passive adjective meaning "able to be [Verbed]".

* **可见** = Visible / Obvious (Able to be seen)
* **可吃** = Edible (Able to be eaten)
* **可信** = Credible (Able to be believed)

When used as a conjunction, it shortens the phrase:
"By looking at [Clause A], [Clause B] **can be seen**."

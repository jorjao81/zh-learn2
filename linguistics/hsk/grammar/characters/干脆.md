# Word Analysis: 干脆 (gāncuì)

## Component Breakdown

### 1. 干 (gān) - Dry

* **Meaning**: Dry, clean, empty of water/superfluous things.
* **Imagery**: A shield (干) or a pestle. Modern meaning focuses on
  "dryness."

### 2. 脆 (cuì) - Crisp

* **Meaning**: Brittle, fragile, crisp, clear (sound).
* **Etymology**: Radical 月 (Flesh/Body) + Phonetic 危 (High/Precarious).
  Originally referred to brittle bones or flesh.
* **Imagery**: Something that breaks cleanly with a snap, rather than
  bending.

## Semantic Composition: 干 (Dry) + 脆 (Crisp)

The metaphor is visceral:

* **Wet/Green Wood**: When you try to break it, it bends, twists, and
  tears. It connects by fibers. It is "hesitant" and "messy."
* **Dry Wood**: When you break it, it snaps instantly into two clean
  pieces. It is "decisive" and "neat."

**干脆** describes a personality or action that has the quality of dry
wood snapping: instant, clean, and without lingering attachments
(hesitation).

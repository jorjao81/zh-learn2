# Character: 千万 (qiānwàn)

## Compound Breakdown: Thousand + Ten Thousand

### 1. 千 (qiān) - Thousand

* **Meaning:** The number 1,000.
* **Visual:** A person (人) with a stroke indicating "one" crossbar, later stylized.

### 2. 万 (wàn) - Ten Thousand

* **Meaning:** The number 10,000.
* **Visual:** Originally a pictogram of a scorpion (with claws and tail). It was borrowed for the sound of the number "ten thousand" because scorpions were numerous (swarm).
* **Cultural Note:** In Chinese, large numbers are grouped by four zeros (myriads), not three. So **万** is a major unit base.

### The Combination: 千万 (qiānwàn)

Literally "10,000,000".

* **As a Number:** Ten million.
* **As an Adverb:** "Countless."
  * The logic: "Of ten million possibilities/ways, this is the one you must choose."
  * Or: "I beseech you with the weight of ten million words."
  * It represents **maximum intensity** of advice.

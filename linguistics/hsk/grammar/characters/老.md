# Character Breakdown: 老 (lǎo)

## Core Semantics

**老 (lǎo)** is a semantic heavyweight in Chinese, functioning as an adjective, an adverb, and a prolific prefix.

## Etymology

* **Classification:** Ideogrammic Compound (会意字).
* **Components:**
  * **耂** (top): A variation of strict hair/elderly radical. Represents long hair of an elder.
  * **匕** (bottom): Originally a transformation of **人** (person), representing a person leaning or changing shape with age. Often interpreted as a cane.
* **Original Meaning:** Old, aged (70 years old usually).

## Semantic Shift: The "Depth" of Oldness

The shift from "old" to its many other meanings follows a logic of **depth, duration, and intensity**.

1. **Old (Time):**
    * **老人** (lǎorén) - old person
    * **古老** (gǔlǎo) - ancient

2. **Experienced / Expert (Depth of Skill):**
    * Someone who has done something for a long time is skilled.
    * **老师** (lǎoshī) - teacher (venerable master)
    * **老手** (lǎoshǒu) - old hand, veteran
    * **老练** (lǎoliàn) - seasoned, experienced

3. **Tough (Texture):**
    * Food that is "old" becomes tough or overcooked.
    * 肉**老**了 (ròu lǎo le) - The meat is tough (overcooked).
    * Vegetables that grow too long become fibrous: 玉米**老**了.

4. **Always / Constantly (Duration -> Frequency):**
    * A state that is "old" is one that persists and doesn't change.
    * **老**迟到 (lǎo chídào) - "Old-ly" late → Always late.

5. **The "Respectful/Familiar" Prefix:**
    * Used to make monosyllabic surnames or animals sound more substantial (bisyllabic) and familiar.
    * **老**王 (Lǎo Wáng) - Old Wang (familiar address).
    * **老**虎 (lǎohǔ) - Tiger.
    * **老**鼠 (lǎoshǔ) - Mouse.
    * *Note: In "Tiger" and "Mouse", 老 has lost its meaning entirely and serves only a phonetic/rhythmic function.*

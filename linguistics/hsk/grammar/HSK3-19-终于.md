# HSK3-19 终于 (Finally / At Last)

## 1. The Core Concept: "The End of a Struggle"

**终于** (zhōngyú) isn't just "finally" in the sense of a sequence (first, next, finally). It carries an emotional weight of **relief** or **conclusion** after a long period of waiting, effort, or difficulty.

It implies: **Process + Difficulty/Delay -> Result.**

## 2. Syntax & Usage

*Structure:* `Subject + 终于 + Verb Phrase`

### A. Successful Outcome (Most Common)
* 经过三年的努力，他**终于**考上了大学。
    (Jīngguò sān nián de nǔlì, tā **zhōngyú** kǎoshàng le dàxué.)
  *After three years of hard work, he **finally** got into university.*

### B. The Long Wait (Relief)
* 你**终于**来了！我等你半天了。
    (Nǐ **zhōngyú** lái le! Wǒ děng nǐ bàntiān le.)
  *You're **finally** here! I've been waiting for you for ages.*

### C. Negative Outcomes (Fatalism)
While usually positive, it can mark the end of a long struggle that ends badly.
* 虽然医生尽力了，病人**终于**还是去世了。
    (Suīrán yīshēng jìnlì le, bìngrén **zhōngyú** háishì qùshì le.)
  *Although the doctors tried their best, the patient **eventually** passed away.*
  *(Note: This usage feels heavier and more fatalistic.)*

## 3. Comparison: 终于 vs. 最后

English speakers often confuse these because both translate to "finally" or "in the end."

| Word | Pinyin | Nuance | Usage |
|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **终于** | zhōngyú | **Emotional/Subjective.** Emphasizes the difficult process and the eventual result. | "At long last!" (Relief) |
| **最后** | zuìhòu | **Chronological/Objective.** Simply the last step in a sequence. | "Last / In the end." |

* **Wrong:** 洗脸，刷牙，**终于**睡觉。(Wash face, brush teeth, *at long last* sleep.) -> Too dramatic for a daily routine.
* **Right:** 洗脸，刷牙，**最后**睡觉。(Wash face, brush teeth, *lastly* sleep.)

* **Right:** 我做完作业，**终于**可以睡觉了！(I finished homework, *finally* I can sleep!) -> Implies the homework was exhausting.

## 4. Key Constraint
**终于** is an adverb, so it appears **before the verb**.
* *Wrong:* **终于**，他来了。 (English style: "Finally, he came.")
* *Right:* 他**终于**来了。 (He finally came.)

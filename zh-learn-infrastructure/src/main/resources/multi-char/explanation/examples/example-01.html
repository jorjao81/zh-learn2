Origin: Compound Word (复合词)

The modern medical term 静脉jìngmài combines: 静 (quiet, still) + 脉 (vessel, channel). The first character, 静jìng, describes a state of calmness or lack of movement. The second character, 脉mài, refers to the vessels that carry blood and, in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), the channels through which Qi (气) flows.

This compound was formed based on anatomical and physiological observations. It follows a descriptive (attributive) + noun structure, where "quiet" modifies "vessel" to describe a specific type of blood vessel.

Usage and Comparison

How to Use 静脉 (jìngmài):

Use 静脉 when referring specifically to the veins that carry blood towards the heart. It is a precise anatomical term.

  • Example sentence: 护士在病人的手臂上找到了合适的静脉进行抽血。 (Hùshì zài bìngrén de shǒubì shàng zhǎodàole héshì de jìngmài jìnxxíng chōuxuè.) - "The nurse found a suitable vein on the patient's arm to draw blood."
  • Common collocations: 静脉曲张jìngmài qūzhāng (varicose veins), 深静脉shēn jìngmài (deep vein).

Comparison: 静脉 (jìngmài) vs. 动脉 (dòngmài)

To fully grasp the meaning of 静脉, it's essential to compare it with its counterpart, 动脉dòngmài (artery).

Feature 静脉 (jìngmài) - Vein 动脉 (dòngmài) - Artery
Etymology 静 (jìng) - quiet, still. Named for its smooth, non-pulsating blood flow. 动 (dòng) - moving, active. Named for the palpable pulse caused by the heart's pumping action.
Function Carries deoxygenated blood toward the heart (with the exception of the pulmonary vein). Carries oxygenated blood away from the heart (with the exception of the pulmonary artery).
Pressure & Flow Low pressure, slower and smoother flow. High pressure, pulsatile flow.

This clear opposition in naming—quiet vessel vs. moving vessel—is a powerful mnemonic device for remembering the function and characteristics of each.
